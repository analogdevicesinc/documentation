EVAL-ADM2867E-ARDZ Arduino Shield
=================================

| Use the **<fc #ff0000>EVAL-ADM2867E-ARDZ</fc>** Arduino Shield with the widely available Arduino UNO and other Arduino compatible devices to easily evaluate <del>\ *5 kV rms* </del> **5.7 kV rms** :adi:`ADM2867E <en/products/adm2867e.html>` isolated RS-485/RS-422 transceiver with low radiated emissions, integrated, isolated dc-to-dc converter with ESD protection on the RS-485 A, B, Y, and Z bus pins:

-  // ≥ ±12 kV IEC 61000-4-2 contact discharge//
-  // ≥ ±15 kV IEC 61000-4-2 air discharge//

|

| **<fc #ff0000>EVAL-ADM2867E-ARDZ</fc>** also features the :adi:`ADP7104 <en/products/adp7104.html>`. The :adi:`ADP7104 <en/products/adp7104.html>` is a CMOS, low dropout linear regulator that operates from 3.3 V to 20 V and provides up to 500 mA of output current.

The **<fc #ff0000>EVAL-ADM2867E-ARDZ</fc>** board gives user the flexibility to choose from an Arduino shield or a USB source for the communicating with the :adi:`ADM2867E <en/products/adm2867e.html>` RS-485 transceiver. As a result, this board can be powered up from an external 5V supply, USB or even from the Arduino and allows for either 5V or 3.3V logic. It also has a switchable Full/Half duplex capability.

Other Key Features
==================

Cable Inversion
---------------

| The :adi:`ADM286xE <en/products/adm2867e.html>` family features a cable inversion functionality to correct errors during installation. This adjustment can be implemented in software on the controller driving the RS485 transceiver and helps avoid additional installation costs to fix wiring errors.
| The :adi:`ADM2867E <en/products/adm2867e.html>` features separate digital logic pins, **INVD** and **INVR**, to correct cases where the driver, receiver, or both are wired in reverse. The **INVD** pin is used to correct driver functionality when Y and Z are wired with the incorrect polarity. Whereas, the **INVR** pin is to correct receiver functionality when A and B are wired with the incorrect polarity.
| The :adi:`ADM2861E <en/products/adm2861e.html>`/:adi:`ADM2865E <en/products/adm2865e.html>` are half-duplex devices that only have a single inversion pin, **INV**, to correct both transmitter and receiver polarity. When the receiver is inverted, the device maintains a Logic 1 receiver output with a 30 mV noise margin when inputs are shorted together or during an open circuit condition.

Low EMI Integrated DC-to-DC Converter
-------------------------------------

| The :adi:`ADM2861E <en/products/adm2861e.html>`/:adi:`ADM2863E <en/products/adm2863e.html>`/:adi:`ADM2865E <en/products/adm2865e.html>`/:adi:`ADM2867E <en/products/adm2867e.html>` also include a flexible integrated dc-to-dc converter optimized for low radiated emissions (EMI) which eliminates the need for an external isolated power supply.
| The isolated dc-to-dc converter is constructed from a set of chip scale co-planar coils separated by an insulating material. By exciting the upper coil with an ac signal, power is magnetically coupled across the isolation barrier where it is rectified and regulated. Because no direct electrical connection exists between the top and bottom coil, the primary and secondary side of the device remain galvanically isolated. This isolated dc-to-dc converter features a regulated output of either 3.3 V or 5 V, selectable via the **VSEL** logic pin, which allows the user to optimize the supply rail of the RS-485 transceiver. For lower power applications, a 3.3 V supply can be chosen. For applications requiring a large differential output voltage, such as PROFIBUS®, the isolated dc-to-dc converter can be operated with a 5 V output. The integrated dc-to-dc converter is optimized to minimize radiated EMI, and allows designers to meet the CISPR32 and EN 55032 Class B requirements on a 2-layer PCB with the addition of two low cost, surface-mount device (SMD) ferrites. <fc #87ceeb>\ *See Foot notes*\ </fc>(( *CISPR32 and EN 55032 are Standards for Electromagnetic Emissions & Electromagnetic compatibility of multimedia equipment.*))

Jumper Configuration
====================

+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| \**Jumper \*\* | Configuration                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
+================+==============================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================+
| **P5**         | The jumper configuration on P5 selects the RE (Receiver-Enable) signal source for the :adi:`ADM2867E <en/products/adm2867e.html>`. To enable it, connect jumper between P5.7 (pin 7 of the P5 header) and P5.8 (connects it to GND). To disable it, connect the jumper between P5.6 and P5.7 (connects it to VCC). To use an Arduino as a source for RE signal, connect the jumper between P5.3 and P5.4 and finally, to use the DE (Driver Enable) as the source for the RE signal, connect the jumper between P5.1 and P5.2. This ensures that when driver is enabled, receiver is disabled and vice versa.                                                |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **P8**         | The jumper configuration on P8 selects the DE (Driver-Enable) signal source for the :adi:`ADM2867E <en/products/adm2867e.html>`. To disable the driver, connect the jumper between P8.5 and P8.6. To use Arduino as a source for DE signal, connect the jumper between P8.3 and P8.4. To use the converted DE signal from the USB to RS485 converter, connect the jumper between P8.1 and P8.2. With no jumper inserted on the P8 pin header, DE is pulled up to VCC and therefore, enabled by default.                                                                                                                                                      |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **P9**         | The jumper configuration on P9 selects the interface. Input_Sel choses where the data for the RS-485 isolator comes from ( can be either USB or from the Arduino Device). If Arduino is chosen as a source for data, connect the jumper between P9.3 and P9.4. if USB connector provides the source for data, connect the jumper between P9.1 and P9.2. To let the Arduino choose the source, connect P9.5 and P9.6.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **P10-P11**    | The jumper configuration on P10 & P11 selects the operation mode of the transceiver (half duplex or full duplex). If both jumpers are inserted connecting A/Y (jumper connected between P10.1 and P10.2) and B/Z (jumper connected between P11.1 and P11.2) then the device operates in the half duplex mode. If both jumpers are disconnected, the device operates in the full duplex mode. **Make sure to either connect both jumpers or disconnect both**.                                                                                                                                                                                                |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **P12**        | The jumper configuration on P12 select the 5V external source. VIN from the Barrel Jack is selected as the source if the jumper is connected between P12.1 and P12.2. USB_5V from the USB port is selected if jumper is connected between P12.3 and P12.4.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **P13**        | The jumper configuration on P13 selects the supply voltage VCC. You can choose to power it up using 5V (externally from the Barrel jack or the USB by connecting the jumper between P13.1 and P13.2 in addition to adding jumpers on P12(see notes next to P12). Alternatively, it could be powered using 5V from the Arduino if the jumper between P13.5 and P13.6 is connected or 3.3V from the Arduino if the jumper between P13.7 and P13.8 is connected. Lastly, you can choose to power the device from a 3.3V LDO by not inserting any jumper on the P13 header. This LDO can be powered by an 5V external power or from the 5V rail from an Arduino. |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **P14**        | The jumper configuration on P14 selects the isolator's output voltage, VISO. Connecting VSEL to VISO sets it at 5V. To do that, connect the jumper between P14.1 and P14.2. Connecting VSEL to GNDISO sets it at 3.3V. To connect it to GNDISO, connect the jumper between P14.3 and P14.4.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **P15**        | The jumper configuration on P15 selects the logic level for the FT232R IC. You can set it to 3.3V by connecting the jumper between P15.3 and P15.4 or set it to 5V by connecting the jumper between P15.2 and P15.3.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
+----------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
