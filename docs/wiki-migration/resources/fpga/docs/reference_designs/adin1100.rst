ADI1100 HDL Reference Design
============================

Introduction
------------

The ADIN110 in an industrial low power 10BASE-T1L Ethernet physical layer (PHY). The PHY sends/receives data to/from the MAC through RGMII, MII or RMII interfaces.

MII interface
-------------

The MII interface is capable of supporting data rates of 100 Mbps and 10 Mbps.

.. image:: https://wiki.analog.com/_media/resources/fpga/docs/reference_designs/adi1100_mii.svg
   :align: center

| 
| The PHY sends a free-running clock as the receive clock(RX_CLK).
| The transmit clock is also a free-running clock generated by the PHY.
| Based on the link speed, the frequency of the Rx/Tx clock generated by the ADIN1100 is 25 MHz for 100 Mbit/s and 2.5 MHz for 10 Mbit/s.

Typically the PS/HPS MAC only exposes a GMII interface. But we can easily obtain the required MII signals. The MII being a subset of the GMII interface. The GMII contains the following extra signals:

-  GTXCLK- Clock signal for gigabit TX signals(125MHz)
-  an extra 4 MSBs, for both RXD and TXD data buses

The reset signal for ADIN1100 comes from the PS/HPS.

There is an optional link_st signal, that indicates if a valid link is established. This signal is connected to the gpio[35].

RMII interface
--------------

.. image:: https://wiki.analog.com/_media/resources/fpga/docs/reference_designs/adi1100_rmii.svg
   :align: center

The RMII interface is capable of supporting data rates of 100 Mbps and 10 Mbps. Its advantage over the MII is the reduced number of signals required.

The reference clock operates at 50MHz in both 100 Mbit/s and 10 Mbit/s mode. In this design both the MAC(MII to RMII converter) and PHY work on the same 50MHz clock.

RGMII interface
---------------

The RGMII interface is capable of supporting data rates of 1 Gbps, 100 Mbps, and 10 Mbps. The benefit of the RGMII over GMII, is that the RGMII consists only of 12 pins, as opposed to GMII's 24.

.. image:: https://wiki.analog.com/_media/resources/fpga/docs/reference_designs/adi1100_rgmii.svg
   :align: center

| 
| Source synchronous interfaces are used for both RX and TX.
| The ADIN1100 generates a 125 MHz, 25 MHz, or 2.5 MHz RXC signal to synchronize the RXD pins.
| The transmit clock signal is provided by the MAC on the TXC line.

The reset signal for ADIN1100 comes from the PS/HPS.
